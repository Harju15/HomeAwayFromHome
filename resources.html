Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is respite care?
Respite care is short-term, temporary care for a child in foster care. It provides a safe place to stay while a long-term placement is arranged, or gives foster families a planned break so placements stay strong.
How long can a child stay at HAH?
Stays range from a single night to several weeks, depending on the child's needs and the agency's placement timeline. Every stay is coordinated with the referring caseworker.
Who can make a referral?
Social workers, case managers, county DSS staff, licensed agencies, and foster parents (through their agency) can all initiate a referral using our online form or by phone.
What ages do you serve?
We serve children and youth in foster care from infancy through age 17. Let us know the child's age in your referral and we'll confirm current availability.
Is HAH licensed?
Yes. HAH operates as a licensed home environment and works within North Carolina child-welfare requirements, in coordination with state and county agencies.
How quickly can a placement happen?
Emergency referrals are often placed within 24 hours, subject to availability. Planned respite can be scheduled in advance — the earlier you reach out, the better we can prepare.
